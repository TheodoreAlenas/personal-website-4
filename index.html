Generally I prefer teams where we talk about everyday things. I'm also into fast builds, incremental build systems that often run fast, fast automated tests that bypass integration and other means to try out a piece of code quickly. If there are multiple teams and one of them can try out a piece of code comfortably, I'd probably prefer to be in that team. Finally, I prefer working in person, in an office, because it's easier to strike a short conversation. You can tell when someone is focused or their eyes are drifting, you can glance at a monitor and you can observe the other person's reaction when you do something. It definitely depends on the team as well.
