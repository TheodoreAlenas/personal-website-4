  6. Preferences

    Generally I prefer social teams. Yes, nerdy teams are open and inclusive but I have bad memories working with them. Social people may be extra loud, some have a lot of energy, sometimes they make fun of me but I'm fine with all of that. I'm also into fast builds, incremental build systems that often run fast, fast automated tests that bypass integration and other means to try out a piece of code quickly. If there are multiple teams and one of them can try out a piece of code comfortably, I'd probably prefer that team. Finally, I prefer working in person, in an office, because it's easier to strike a short conversation. You can tell when someone is focused or their eyes are drifting, you can glance at a monitor and you can observe the other person's reaction when you do something. It definitely depends on the team as well.
